Not Just Another Hackathon.

Maker Block Party is a preliminary communal festival. We are bringing together students across Art, Media, and History to build tools and ideas alongside STEM majors.

Our goal is to align students with a network of peers, mentors, and careers without the typical "burnout" culture of tech events.

  • Interdisciplinary engagement between Art & STEM departments.
  • Workshops, demos, and keynotes from faculty & industry.
  • Connecting students to local makerspaces and career networks.

The Experience

We've designed the event to be humane, creative, and social.

24-Hour Internet Café

The social spine of the event. A welcoming commons with coffee, tea, and snacks. Designed for endurance without burnout. A place to drop in, work, and chat.

Idea Exchange & Keynotes

Short invited talks and visiting artists present for informal conversation. Not just lectures, but a dialogue about the tools we build.

Booths & Demos

A dedicated area for visiting talent, local makerspaces, and student clubs to demonstrate their work live.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this a competition?

While competition is not the primary focus for this first iteration, we are exploring options! The main goal is collaboration and connection. However, we may integrate with existing events like Cadathon.

Do I need to be an engineer?

Absolutely not! This event is specifically designed to bring Art students, Media majors, and Historians together with STEM students. We need creatives, designers, and thinkers just as much as coders.

What should I bring?

Bring your laptop, any specific art supplies you love, and an open mind. We will provide Wi-Fi, power, coffee, and a supportive environment.
